## 1. Arithmetic Verification
The rebuttal proposes correcting the "Relative Risk" (RR) using two factors:
1.  **Over-Identification Factor ($O$)**: 2.5x
2.  **Under-Registration Factor ($U$)**: 2x to 4x

The formula for the corrected risk is:
$$ \text{Corrected RR} = \frac{\text{Reported RR}}{O \times U} $$

Using Cremieux's reported RR of **12.59x**:

- **Scenario A (2x Under-Registration)**:
  $$ \frac{12.59}{2.5 \times 2} = \frac{12.59}{5} = \mathbf{2.52\times} $$
  *(Matches Rebuttal Claim)*

- **Scenario B (4x Under-Registration)**:
  $$ \frac{12.59}{2.5 \times 4} = \frac{12.59}{10} = \mathbf{1.26\times} $$

## 2. Source Validation

### Claim 1: "Professionals over-identified pit bulls by 2.5x"
- **Source**: [Olson KR, Levy JK, et al. "Inconsistent identification of pit bull-type dogs by shelter staff." *The Veterinary Journal*, 2015.](http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S109002331500310X)
- **Findings**: The study found that shelter staff identified **52%** of study dogs as "pit bull-type", while DNA analysis confirmed only **21%** possessed significant pit bull heritage.
- **Validation**: $52 / 21 \approx 2.48$. This supports the **2.5x** over-identification factor.

### Claim 2: "Pit bulls are less than 5% of NYC licenses... National estimates indicate 10-20%"
- **NYC Data**: Analysis of [NYC DOHMH licensing data](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Health/NYC-Dog-Licensing-Dataset/nu7n-tubp) confirms Pit Bulls reported as **~5.1% to 5.5%** of the licensed population.
- **National Data**: Various estimates place the "Pit Bull type" population between **10-20%** nationally. One estimate suggests Pit Bulls are ~20% of the U.S. dog population (often cited from sources like *dogbitelaw.com* or shelter intake data). A more conservative DNA-based estimate often cited is ~14%.
- **Validation**:
  - If NYC actual population is ~10% (conservative national average) and reported is 5%: Under-registration is **2x**.
  - If NYC actual population is ~20% (higher national estimate) and reported is 5%: Under-registration is **4x**.
  - The "Housing Ban/Insurance" incentive argument provides a logical mechanism for this specific under-registration relative to other breeds like Maltese.

## Summary
